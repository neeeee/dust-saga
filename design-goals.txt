Dust Saga is a re-imagining of the classic 2007 Japanese MMORPG Pandora Saga.
It is a large scale PVP focused game, with a relatively small overworld.

There are 3 nations connected by a central area called Astir.
Astir has 4 city zones (including Central Plaza) and 3 paths with enemies leading to nation-specific areas.

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Non-nation maps are all PVP zones, excluding the Seicaville map when the war isn't active, and players are simply visiting their guild castle.

PVP zones are:

Muldia - general PvEvP map with regular enemies and bosses for all players to fight. Players can kill opposing nation members, noted by their red aura to indicate being an enemy, who can't read opposing nation say, party, or shout chat. Enemies cannot be privately messaged in any PVP zone.

El Behd - PvEvP map more focused on leveling with multiple zones fit for killing large mob pulls. Muldia and El Behd have exits to each other at a few points on their maps.

Ridelium War Zone - 48 v 48 v 48 player war zone only available during scheduled war time. Players fight for control over towers on the map, aiming to own all of them at the end of the 1 hour war timer.

The course of the war 
 
30 minutes before the war begins is used to form parties and plan strategies.

 
The outcome of a war is determined by the number of power points.
Power points are increased in the following ways:
- Obtaining bases triggers hourly bugle calls
- Defeating enemy players

 
*A Bugle Call is the time when faction points are tallied at regular 10 minute intervals.
Rankings change at this time.

 
Additionally, your power points will decrease in the following ways:
• If you do not occupy any bases when a Bugle Call occurs.
• If you are defeated by an enemy nation.
• If you are automatically revived after death.

 
*Point increases or decreases for knocking down or being knocked down are also recorded during the bugle call.

 

A base can be captured by destroying the guard towers protecting it on all four sides and destroying the enemy nation's core stone within the base .

The victorious nation will receive a penalty of reduced starting power points in the next war, but
will also receive the benefit of increased item drop rates until the next war.

 
The defeated nation will receive a bonus to its power points in the next war.

 
In addition, regardless of victory or defeat, benefits are granted based on the type and number of forts occupied.

How to register 
You can register for each troop type by talking to the NPC " New War Receptionist
". There are the following troop types, each with its own characteristics and limitations.

Commander-in-Chief 
Only one person will be appointed from each country. If there are multiple candidates, a lottery will be held.
If you die on the battlefield, you can respawn within the war map.
You can choose the location of your main camp during the new war.
During wartime, you can communicate using the commander chat window.
A minimap exclusively for commanders is available, displaying the distribution of their country's forces.
　This distribution map is updated at regular intervals.
The overall map also displays a distribution map of forces, with your party members,
　your country's party leader, and your country's characters color-coded.
　Clicking on your country's party leader's icon
　will allow you to speak to that character via whisper.
deputy commander

*The co-commander chat is a message that reaches all regular and volunteer soldiers of the same nation.
Regular soldiers 
There is a limit to the number of regular soldiers, and if the number of applicants exceeds the limit, a lottery will be held.
If you die on the battlefield, you can respawn at a designated respawn point on the war map, allowing you to always participate in the war.
Regular soldier registration closes 30 minutes before the start of battle.
volunteer soldiers 
If you are unsuccessful in the lottery for regular soldiers, you will become a volunteer soldier.
Volunteers can register to participate at any time during the war.

If you die on the battlefield, you will leave the war map and return to the location you set as your base.
If there are any volunteers waiting in line when you leave, those characters will move to the battlefield in the order they were registered.
Upon entering the new war map, a minimap will appear, allowing you to select your spawn point.

In the Ruined City Three Nations War held the week before the Battle of Siegheim, the nations with the highest overall battle results will be given priority in determining the location of their main base. (Specifications updated on 2009/5/22)

Winning and losing rules 
The basic rules remain the same as in the Ruined City Three Kingdoms War: the increase or decrease in power points is calculated every 10 minutes during bugle calls, and the country with the most power points at the final bugle call wins. However, the new war adds several new victory/defeat conditions.

Victory conditions 
At the time of the bugle call, both enemy nations have zero power points.
Destroy the master cores of both enemy nations.
Capture all bases (victory is achieved once all bases are captured, not during Bugle Call).
 
A country that fulfills any of the victory conditions will achieve victory, and the war will end at that point, even if there is still time remaining.
Defeat conditions 
When Bugle Call is called, your country's influence points are at 0.
The country's master core is destroyed.
 
If a country meets any of the defeat conditions, it will be defeated, and all of its characters will be forcibly removed from the battlefield map.

What is the main camp? 
Each country possesses one main base from the start of the war.
The main base consists of the following elements:

MasterCore 
A Master Core is located within the main base, and its destruction results in immediate defeat.
The Master Core can be destroyed by attacks, and only the enemy country's Master Core can be attacked.
Also, the Master Core cannot be healed.
*Status effects, buffs, and debuffs do not affect the Master Core.

Door (Gate) 
The main base has a door, and destroying it will allow the enemy to enter.
The door can be destroyed by attacking, and only enemy doors can be attacked.
In other words, destroying the enemy's door will allow you to enter their main base, and as long as enemy soldiers do not destroy your own door, they will not be able to enter your main base.
*You can freely pass through your own main base.

 
Similar to the Master Core, you cannot heal doors, but you can increase their defense.
The defense of a door changes depending on the number of bases your country occupies (the main base is not included), and the more bases you occupy, the stronger the door's defense becomes .
*Status effects, buffs, and debuffs do not apply.

 
* You cannot use ' Memorize ' or ' Transport ' within the main camp .

What is a base? 
The new war map has 16 bases.
At the start of the war, all bases are unclaimed and have no guard towers.
Each base consists of the following elements:

core 
Destroying a core outside of your own country allows you to occupy that base.

Safewall 
The safe wall serves to block entry points into the base, preventing enemy soldiers from entering until the safe wall disappears.
The safe wall disappears when all guard towers are destroyed, but it will reappear if even one guard tower is rebuilt.
*Characters of the occupying nation can pass through freely.

Guard tower 
Two safe walls are automatically constructed when you capture a base.
You can also use specific base-specific items (see below for details on base-specific items) to construct or repair them.
The safe wall disappears when all constructed guard towers are destroyed.

Respawn Crystal 
By using specific base-specific items ( see below for details on base - specific items
), you can activate a respawn crystal and place a respawn point within your base.
However, if an enemy nation captures your base, the respawn crystal will cease to function, and the respawn point within the base will disappear.

 
Unlike the Tower of the Ruined City Three Kingdoms, the bases on the new war map allow you to recover your core, construct and recover guard towers, and set up respawn points.

Core auto-recovery 
If there are characters from your country (excluding those in a dead state) within your base grounds, all cores within the base will automatically regenerate at regular intervals.
The amount of regeneration increases depending on the number of characters from your country within the base grounds
. However, cores that have been destroyed will not automatically regenerate.

Construction of guard towers 
You can construct a guard tower by accumulating a certain number of construction points using the base item " Shellneat Ore ? ". You can use it by clicking on the base boxes
located in the four corners of your base while you have the base item in your possession .

 
If you build a guard tower after all existing guard towers have been destroyed, a safe wall will reappear.
Guard towers cannot be built at bases that are not owned by a nation or that are occupied by an enemy nation.
However, if a base is occupied by an enemy nation, the construction points will be reset to 0.

Guard Tower Recovery 
You can repair guard towers by using the base item " Curelight Ore ? ".

 
When you possess a base item, you can use it by clicking on the base box located in the four corners of your base .
The amount of healing is a fixed value set for each item, and the healing is averaged out according to the number of guard towers that are damaged at the time of use.

Example: In a base with two damaged guard towers, using an item that heals 4000 LP
　　would restore 4000 / 2 = 2000 LP per tower.

Setting up respawn points 
By using the base item " Crystal Tear ? " and accumulating a certain number of activation points, you can set up a respawn point within your base. You can use it by clicking the Respawn Crystal ?
while possessing the base item .

 
Setting a respawn point allows you to select it as a location where regular soldiers can respawn if they die.
However, if the base is captured by an enemy nation, the activation point will reset to 0, and if you had activated a respawn crystal , it will cease to function, and the respawn point will disappear.

* You cannot use ' Memorize ' or ' Transport ' within the base .

The skill system is based on point allocation at certain thresholds. Exact values will be determined at a later time in development. Generally, the idea is:

Example - Paladin:
Slash - starts 5 points / 80 cap
Cleave - starts 0 points / 61 cap
Bash - starts 0 points / 50 cap
Defend - starts 8 points / 80 cap

5 slash = slice
16 slash = double slice
21 slash = whirling strike
....etc

If a class other than paladin can get 21 slash, like a Shadowblade, it can also learn whirling strike. To balance this, not all weapons can be equipped equally. General rules like mages being unable to wield melee weapons save for rare exceptions, and those exceptions have balancing factors. 

A melee mage, Shadowblade, can equip a 1 handed sword and shield, but can't equip a kite shield - they can only equip small round shields with lower defense values. They can't equip plate or leather armor either. Their defense comes from careful play and spell shields used well.
